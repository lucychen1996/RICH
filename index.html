RICHARD'S IMAGINE CENTER OF HOPE, INC (RICH) AT A GLANCE.

Here at Richard's Imagine Center of Hope, Inc (RICH) your dreams can be revealed even through a clouded vision. We serve individuals from the City of Pittsburgh, Allegheny County. We are driven in many ways by personal experiences, humility, and pride to influence people’s lives from a grassroot, community-based level. Our decision making process is based on providing guidance and support to people who are looking for a second or third time to get their life in perspective. We strive to build productive relationships with our stakeholders and make a positive impact in the pursuit of happiness.

ORGANIZATION INFORMATION

Richard's Imagine Center of Hope, Inc. (HOPE) will provide temporary housing, educational/ vocational support, and counseling to individuals who are seeking permanent housing, educational resources, employment options, mental/physical guidance, and financial freedom towards a brighter future. The organization will serve as a citywide network and referral service for education systems, social service agencies, and community organizations; operate an information and education center for college readiness and workforce development practices, and promote social justice awareness for the citizens of the City of Pittsburgh. It will serve as a referral and support system to address people's academic, financial, social, emotional, and mental needs. The center will address homelessness, high school dropouts, housing, youth development, career development, teen parenting, community pride, and civic engagement. The target group is individuals needing a second or third chance to live a normal life with normal circumstances, interested in career development, willing to improve the quality of life for themselves and those whom they care for, and eager to learn and grow with adequate support systems in place. If I had to put an age range for my target group it would be 13-25. The center is willing to work with individuals who are serious about the outcomes of their futures. Not just talking the talk but walking the walk.

VISION

To increase young people’s access to safe and stable housing, school/employment connections, enhance social and emotional well-being, self-sufficiency, help individuals identify their capabilities and build their careers, become more involved with community development, and work towards building permanent, lasting relationships with families, communities, schools, and other social networks, all positive of course.

YOUTH DEVELOPMENT

RICH’s target market is individuals ages 13-25 who dropped out of school, lives on the streets, faces teen parenting, t, seeking college guidance/scholarships, and possibly exposed to neglect, violence, and/or trauma. The intentional market is low to moderate income households, local businesses, non-profit organizations, and government entities within Allegheny County, City of Pittsburgh. Services will consist of job training/placement, housing security, community development, educational/vocational training, counseling, mentoring, social skill support, civic engagement, referral support, post-secondary focus, employment options, relationship building, credit counseling, and gardening/landscaping. Activities will focus on career building classes, education workshops/seminars, job shadowing, mock interviews, industry trips, community outreach, GED/pre-apprentice/vocational classes, college tours/visits, job readiness development, and more.
